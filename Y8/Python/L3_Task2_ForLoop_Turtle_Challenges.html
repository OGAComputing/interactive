Draw a Square
★
🎯 Your goal: Use a for loop to draw a square — 4 sides of equal length.
The turtle should move forward, turn 90°, and repeat 4 times.
📐 Remember: A square has 4 sides and 4 corners. At each corner the turtle turns 90°. That's 4 × 90° = 360° — a full turn!
Your loop needs range(4) and the turn must be 90 degrees.
Show me a hint 
import turtle
t = turtle.Turtle()

for i in range(4):
    t.forward(100)   # change 100 if you like
    t.left(90)

turtle.done()

Draw a Triangle
★★
🎯 Your goal: Use a for loop to draw an equilateral triangle — 3 equal sides.
Hint: angle = 360 ÷ 3 = 120°. Your loop needs range(3).
📐 The maths: For any regular shape: angle = 360 ÷ number of sides.
Triangle: 360 ÷ 3 = 120°. So each time the turtle completes a side, it turns 120°.
Show me a hint
Start with the same template as the square but change two numbers: range(3) (3 sides) and 120 degrees (360 ÷ 3 = 120). 
for i in range(3):
    t.forward(100)
    t.left(120)

Coloured Pentagon
★★★
🎯 Your goal: Draw a pentagon (5 sides) AND change the pen colour with pencolor().
angle = 360 ÷ 5 = 72°. Your loop needs range(5).
🖌 Adding colour: Add t.pencolor("red") (or any colour name) before your loop starts.
Try: "blue", "green", "purple", "orange", "yellow", "cyan", "pink"
Show me a hint 
import turtle
t = turtle.Turtle()
t.pencolor("blue")   # add colour!
t.pensize(3)

for i in range(5):
    t.forward(100)
    t.left(72)       # 360 ÷ 5

turtle.done()

Spiral Pattern ⭐ Extension
★★★★
🎯 Your goal: Make the turtle draw a spiral by increasing the forward distance each loop using i.
Use t.forward(i * 10) and a slightly-off angle like 91 to create the spiral effect.
🔑 The key idea: Each time the loop runs, i gets bigger (0, 1, 2, 3...). If you use i * 10 as the forward distance, the steps get longer each time — creating a spiral!
t.speed(0) makes it draw instantly.
Show me a hint 
import turtle
t = turtle.Turtle()
t.speed(0)

for i in range(36):
    t.forward(i * 10)
    t.left(91)

turtle.done()
Try changing: the range, the multiplier (10), or the angle (91). What happens?
