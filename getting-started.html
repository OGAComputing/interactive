Getting Started

A guide for teachers: setting up Google Classroom integration with the CS Activities Hub

← Back to Activities Hub
What does this integration do? The OGA CS Activities Hub can automatically post student scores back to your Google Classroom gradebook when students complete activities. This guide walks you through the one-time setup required to enable that. Once set up, grades flow automatically — no manual marking needed.
Before you begin
PRE

What you need

  • A Google account associated with your school (e.g. yourname@outwood.com)
  • At least one active class in Google Classroom
  • A desktop browser (Chrome recommended) — this setup does not work on mobile
Note Each teacher in the department completes this setup once with their own account to get their own personal proxy. You cannot share a proxy between teachers.
Setup steps
1

Deploy your personal grade proxy

This is the most important step. It creates a small Google Apps Script in your Google Drive that securely submits grades to your classes on your behalf.

  1. Open the Grade Proxy Setup page
  2. Click Sign in with Google and use your school account
  3. Grant the requested permissions — these allow the setup page to create a script project in your Drive
  4. The page will automatically create and deploy the script — this takes about 10–15 seconds
  5. When complete, click Activate → to open the proxy URL in a new tab
  6. In that new tab, Google will ask you to grant the proxy permission to access your Classroom — click Allow
  7. Close that tab and return to the setup page
Open Setup Page →
Important This step saves your proxy URL to this browser. If you switch to a different browser or device, you will need to re-run setup on that browser — the process only takes a minute.
Where is the script saved? In your Google Drive under My Drive → Apps Scripts → OGA Classroom Grade Proxy. You can view or delete it there at any time.
2

Sign in on the Activities Hub

Head to the CS Activities Hub. In the Teacher Tools bar at the top of the page:

  1. Click Sign in to Assign to Classroom
  2. Sign in with the same school Google account you used in Step 1
  3. Grant the requested permissions (read your courses, manage coursework)

Once signed in, a class selector dropdown will appear and each activity card will show an Assign to Class button.

3

Assign an activity to your class

  1. Use the class selector in the Teacher Tools bar to choose which class you are assigning to
  2. Find the activity you want on the hub (use the Year tabs to filter)
  3. Click ➕ Assign to Class on the activity card

The platform will create a new assignment in Google Classroom with the activity link automatically embedded. The assignment appears in your students' Classroom feeds immediately.

How does grade sync work? The assignment link includes a ?courseId= parameter. When a student opens the link and signs in, the platform can identify their submission and post their score automatically.
4

Students complete the activity

Students open the assignment from their Google Classroom feed. The activity page will:

  1. Show a sign-in banner at the top of the page
  2. Ask the student to sign in with their school Google account
  3. Allow the student to complete the activity as normal
  4. On completion, automatically submit their score as a draft grade in Classroom
Draft grades Scores appear as draft grades in Classroom — you still need to review and Return them to students in the Classroom gradebook, just as you would with any manually marked assignment.
5

Return grades to students

Once students have completed the activity, their draft grades will be visible in your Classroom gradebook:

  1. Open the assignment in Google Classroom
  2. Go to the Student Work tab
  3. Review the draft grades — you can adjust any score if needed
  4. Select all students and click Return to publish grades

Troubleshooting
Problem Fix
"No proxy configured" error when a student completes an activity Re-run Step 1 on the current browser, or append ?proxyUrl=YOUR_URL to the activity link when creating the assignment.
Grades not appearing in Classroom Check the Apps Script deployment is still active: open Google Drive → My Drive → Apps Scripts → OGA Classroom Grade Proxy → Manage Deployments.
Activity cards show no "Assign to Class" button You need to sign in first — click Sign in to Assign to Classroom in the Teacher Tools bar on the hub page.
Class dropdown is empty after signing in Check that your Google Classroom courses are set to Active (not Archived). Archived courses are not shown.
Switched to a new computer or browser Re-run the setup page on the new browser — it only takes a minute and your previous proxy is replaced with a new one automatically.
Setup page fails with a permissions error Make sure you are signed in with a Google account that has access to Google Apps Script. Some school accounts may have Apps Script disabled by the IT administrator — contact your IT support team.
